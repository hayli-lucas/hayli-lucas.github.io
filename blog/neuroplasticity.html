Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is described as the mind’s ability to form connections, change and adapt. It can be compared to a city and its roads (your brain and the connections it forms). When new roads are formed, the city runs more efficiently; likewise, your brain becomes stronger and improves itself when you learn a new thing or gain better understanding that creates stronger connections. It is all a part of training your brain and then reinforcing it.

Some live under the false pretence that upon completing mainstream schooling, you are exempted from this training. This could not be further from the truth. If those roads are left to weather, the city becomes more isolated and starved, this would also affect the way your brain responds to receiving new information. It can prove difficult to pick up new information when you have been neglecting your brain its proper training.

In conclusion, keep asking questions and keep seeking answers to continue reenforcing your brain’s infrastructure and when you do learn something new, don’t stop there; if you have the time to do some further research, do it. Not just for the sake of the current assignment of your coding course, but for the sake of your mind and its growth.
